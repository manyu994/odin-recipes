Dal Tadka

Description

Dal Tadka is a popular Indian lentil dish made with toor dal (split pigeon peas) or a mix of lentils, cooked until soft, and then infused with a flavorful tempering of ghee, garlic, onions, tomatoes, and aromatic spices. This comforting dish is best enjoyed with rice or roti and is a staple in Indian households and restaurants alike. The word Tadka refers to the tempering process where mustard seeds, cumin, and other spices are fried in ghee or oil to release their flavors before being poured over the cooked dal. This simple yet rich dish is easy to make and packed with nutrition, making it perfect for a wholesome meal


Ingredients

For The Dal

  • 1 cup toor dal (pigeon peas) or mixed lentils
  • 3 cups water
  • 1 small tomato, chopped
  • 1 small onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • ½ teaspoon turmeric powder
  • ½ teaspoon salt (or to taste)

For the Tadka (Tempering):

  • 2 tablespoons ghee or oil
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 2 cloves garlic, sliced
  • 1-2 dried red chilies
  • ½ teaspoon mustard seeds
  • ½ teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1 pinch asafoetida (hing) (optional)
  • 1 tablespoon chopped coriander leaves (for garnish)


Steps

Cooking the Dal:

  1. Rinse the dal thoroughly in water 2-3 times.
  2. In a pressure cooker or pot, add the dal, 3 cups of water, chopped tomato, onion, minced garlic, turmeric, and salt.
  3. If using a pressure cooker, cook for 3-4 whistles. If using a regular pot, cook until the lentils are soft and mushy
    (about 30-40 minutes), adding water if needed.
  4. Once cooked, mash the dal slightly using the back of a spoon for a creamy texture. Set aside.

Making the Tadka (Tempering):

  1. In a small pan, heat ghee or oil on medium flame.
  2. Add cumin seeds and mustard seeds. Let them crackle.
  3. Add sliced garlic and sauté until golden brown.
  4. Toss in dried red chilies, red chili powder, and asafoetida (hing). Stir for a few seconds.
  5. Pour this hot tempering over the cooked dal and mix well.

Serving

Garnish with fresh coriander leaves and serve hot with steamed rice or roti. Enjoy!
